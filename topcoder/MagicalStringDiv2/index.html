Here is a summary of the problem:

  • You are given a string S, composed solely of the characters '<' and '>'
  • S.size() is even.
  • You are allowed to replace a '<' with a '>', and vice versa.
  • Let k be S.size() / 2.
  • Your goal is to convert S to k '>'s followed by k '<''s.
  • Return the minimum number of replacements than you need to do.
  • Topcoder constrains S.size() between 2 and 50. My testing script has strings up to 1000 in size, and you should be able to do up to about 10,000,000.

The examples

Example S Answer
0 ">><<><" 2
1 ">>>><<<<" 0
2 "<<>>" 4
3 "<><<<>>>>><<>>>>><>><<<>><><><><<><<<<<><<>>><><><" 20

When do you change a character?
  • When its index is less than S.size()/2, and it is not a '>'.
  • When its index is greater than or equal to S.size()/2, and it is not a '<'.
Run through S and add those up.
